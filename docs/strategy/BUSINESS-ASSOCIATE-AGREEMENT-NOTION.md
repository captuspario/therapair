# Business Associate Agreement - Notion

**Covered Entity:** Therapair (Unison Mental Health)  
**Business Associate:** Notion Labs, Inc.  
**Effective Date:** [DATE]  
**Purpose:** Database storage and management of protected health information (PHI)

---

## 1. Definitions

**Covered Entity** means Therapair, a platform operated by Unison Mental Health that provides therapist matching services and handles protected health information.

**Business Associate** means Notion Labs, Inc., which provides database storage and management services to the Covered Entity.

**Protected Health Information (PHI)** means individually identifiable health information that is transmitted or maintained in any form or medium by the Business Associate on behalf of the Covered Entity.

**Required by Law** means a mandate contained in law that compels an entity to make a use or disclosure of PHI and that is enforceable in a court of law.

---

## 2. Permitted Uses and Disclosures

The Business Associate may use or disclose PHI to perform functions, activities, or services for, or on behalf of, the Covered Entity as specified in the underlying service agreement, provided that such use or disclosure would not violate the HIPAA Privacy Rule if done by the Covered Entity.

### 2.1 Specific Permitted Uses
- **Database Storage:** Store PHI in encrypted Notion databases for therapist matching purposes
- **Data Processing:** Process PHI to facilitate matching between clients and therapists
- **Data Retrieval:** Retrieve PHI when requested by authorized Covered Entity personnel
- **Data Backup:** Create and maintain encrypted backups of PHI as part of standard service operations

### 2.2 Prohibited Uses
- Use PHI for any purpose other than those specified in this Agreement
- Disclose PHI to any third party without explicit written authorization from the Covered Entity
- Use PHI for marketing or commercial purposes
- Access PHI for any purpose not related to the services provided under this Agreement

---

## 3. Safeguards

The Business Associate shall implement appropriate safeguards to prevent use or disclosure of PHI other than as provided for by this Agreement.

### 3.1 Administrative Safeguards
- Designate a security officer responsible for the development and implementation of security policies
- Implement workforce training programs on HIPAA compliance and PHI protection
- Establish access management procedures to ensure only authorized personnel access PHI
- Develop incident response procedures for security breaches involving PHI

### 3.2 Physical Safeguards
- Maintain secure data centers with controlled access
- Implement workstation security measures
- Ensure secure disposal of electronic media containing PHI
- Control access to facilities where PHI is stored or processed

### 3.3 Technical Safeguards
- Implement encryption for PHI at rest using AES-256 or equivalent
- Use secure transmission protocols (TLS 1.3 or higher) for PHI in transit
- Implement access controls including unique user identification and multi-factor authentication
- Maintain comprehensive audit logs of all PHI access and modifications

---

## 4. Reporting and Mitigation

### 4.1 Security Incidents
The Business Associate shall report to the Covered Entity any use or disclosure of PHI not provided for by this Agreement, including security incidents, within 24 hours of discovery.

### 4.2 Breach Notification
In the event of a breach of unsecured PHI, the Business Associate shall:
- Notify the Covered Entity within 24 hours of discovery
- Provide detailed information about the breach, including:
  - Nature of the breach
  - PHI involved
  - Individuals affected
  - Steps taken to mitigate the breach
  - Measures implemented to prevent future occurrences

### 4.3 Mitigation
The Business Associate shall take reasonable steps to mitigate any harmful effects of a use or disclosure of PHI that is not permitted by this Agreement.

---

## 5. Subcontractors

The Business Associate shall ensure that any subcontractors that create, receive, maintain, or transmit PHI on behalf of the Business Associate agree to the same restrictions and conditions that apply to the Business Associate with respect to such information.

### 5.1 Subcontractor Requirements
- Execute written agreements containing the same restrictions and conditions as this Agreement
- Implement appropriate safeguards to protect PHI
- Report any security incidents or breaches to the Business Associate
- Comply with all applicable HIPAA requirements

## 7. Termination

### 7.1 Termination for Cause
Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately if the other party breaches a material term of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within 30 days of written notice.

### 7.2 Effect of Termination
Upon termination of this Agreement, the Business Associate shall:
- Return or destroy all PHI received from the Covered Entity
- Retain no copies of such PHI
- If return or destruction is not feasible, extend the protections of this Agreement to such PHI and limit further uses and disclosures to those purposes that make the return or destruction infeasible

## 8. Compliance with HIPAA

The Business Associate shall comply with all applicable requirements of the HIPAA Security Rule (45 CFR Part 160 and Part 164, Subparts A and C) and the HIPAA Privacy Rule (45 CFR Part 160 and Part 164, Subparts A and E).
